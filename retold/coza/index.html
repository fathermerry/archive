    • Thursday, August 22, 2013

      A member of the Commonwealth of Zion Assembly; Ms. Ese Walters, alleged that she has had extra-marital affairs with the Pastor and General Overseer, Pastor Biodun Fatoyinbo. For some reason, Ms. Walters deleted the original post from her blog. The post is reproduced here. However, the follow-up post she did is here. You may also read Ese's response here.

    • This piece of news has generated plenty of buzz, especially on Social Media, as to the culpability of the affected parties in the issue. Many people have written blogposts about the issue. You may read these here, here, and here.

    • This issue sparked a barrage of confessions by many other ladies who claimed to have had affairs with the Pastor.

    • Sunday, August 25, 2013

      Pastor Fatoyinbo, while addressing his church members, promised that, whereas God had told him to keep quiet and not speak on/about the issue, the leadership of the church would consult, and, in the nearest future, release what he termed "A Robust Response"

    • We believe that, while no one has the right to judge a man by his actions and/or inactions, men in positions of authority and trust need to justify the trust reposed in them by their followers. One of the ways through which this trust will be justified, in this case, is through a release of the so-called "robust response" by the man of God.

    • We also believe that everyone who calls himself a christian has a right to know what actually happened between Ms. Walters and Pastor Fatoyinbo. This is because their belief, and faith, is being called into question, and their God, ridiculed.

    • Today

      It has been since the robust response was promised and, in order to keep track of this promise, we have started a countdown here, all in a bid to make sure that this issue isn't forgotten and confined to the dusty shelf of history.
